Digital Certificate and Cl@ve in Spain

A practical guide to Spain’s main digital identity systems, why they matter, and how they help you complete official procedures online.

6 min read

Two different tools

Digital Certificate and Cl@ve can both help with online public services, but they are not the same system.

Quick Answer

Digital Certificate and Cl@ve are used to identify yourself online with Spanish public administrations. They may help with tax, Social Security, health, immigration, municipal and other official procedures, depending on the administration and the specific process. They are not the same system, and many residents eventually need at least one of them.

What is a Digital Certificate?

Digital identity certificate

A Digital Certificate is a digital identity certificate installed or managed on a device or browser.

Official portal access

It can be used to sign in to official government portals where certificates are accepted.

Electronic signing

It may also allow you to sign documents electronically, depending on the portal and procedure.

Common uses

It is commonly used for tax, Social Security and administrative procedures.

Keep it general

Technical setup and accepted browsers can change. Check the official site before applying or installing anything.

What is Cl@ve?

Electronic identification system

Cl@ve is Spain’s electronic identification system for accessing public services.

Access methods

It may use app-based or code-based access depending on the setup and registration level.

Regular online access

Cl@ve is often easier for regular sign-in access across public portals.

Different from a certificate

Cl@ve is not exactly the same as a Digital Certificate and may not be accepted for every certificate-based action.

Important

Do not assume Cl@ve and a Digital Certificate can be used interchangeably. Which one you need depends on the procedure.

What can you use them for?

Tax Agency procedures

They may allow you to access tax data, file or review tax procedures, and manage some tax notifications.

Social Security procedures

They may allow you to check Social Security records, certificates or contribution-related information.

Notifications

Digital access can help you check official notifications where the relevant administration uses online notice systems.

Municipal procedures

Some town halls may allow certificate requests, local tax tasks or other municipal procedures online.

Immigration-related procedures

Some immigration or residence-related processes may be available online where the relevant portal supports them.

Health service access

Regional health services may support online access depending on the autonomous community and procedure.

Certificates and official documents

They may allow downloading certificates or official documents from public portals.

Important

Availability depends on the public administration and the specific procedure. Do not assume every process can be completed online.

Before you start

Details that may be needed

  • NIE.
  • Passport or national ID.
  • Spanish phone number, in some cases.
  • Email address.
  • Appointment or identity verification, depending on the route.
  • Access to official government websites.
Requirements can vary

These details are not always required in the same way. The process may vary by route, document type and public administration.

Real Questions People Ask

Do I need both Digital Certificate and Cl@ve?

Not always. Many residents eventually use at least one, and some find both useful. Which one you need depends on the procedure.

Can I use them without being Spanish?

Often yes, depending on your identity documents and the registration route. Check the official site for the exact requirements that apply to you.

Do I need a NIE?

A NIE is commonly relevant for foreign residents using Spanish public administration online, but exact requirements may vary by system and route.

Can I use them on my phone?

Cl@ve may support app-based access. Digital Certificate use on phones depends on the certificate, device and portal, so check official instructions before relying on it.

What happens if I change computer?

You may need backup, export or reinstallation steps for a Digital Certificate. Save official recovery or export guidance where applicable.

Are they useful for taxes?

Yes. They may allow you to access Tax Agency services, check data, manage notifications or complete tax procedures, depending on the process.

Are they useful for Social Security?

Yes. They may allow access to Social Security services, certificates and records, depending on the service and identification method accepted.
