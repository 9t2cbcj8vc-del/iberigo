Driving Licence in Spain for New Residents

A practical guide to understanding when you can use your existing licence in Spain, when you may need to exchange or renew it, and what new residents should check after moving.

Check your licence type

Driving rules can depend on your country of issue, residency status, licence category, expiry date, medical requirements and current DGT rules.

Quick Answer

If you have an EU or EEA driving licence, you can usually drive in Spain with it while it is valid, but renewal rules may apply once you are resident. If you have a non-EU licence, the answer depends on the country of issue and whether Spain has an exchange agreement. Some residents may need to exchange, renew or register their licence. Always check the current DGT rules for your licence type.

EU/EEA driving licences

Generally recognised

EU and EEA driving licences are generally recognised in Spain while they are valid.

Renewal after residence

If your licence expires while you are resident in Spain, you may need to renew it through Spain.

Spanish rules may apply

Spanish medical and renewal rules may apply depending on your licence, category and circumstances.

Long-validity licences

Long-validity or indefinite licences may have special requirements, so check current DGT guidance.

Avoid tourist assumptions

A licence that is simple to use as a visitor may raise different questions once you become resident in Spain.

Non-EU driving licences

Temporary use may be limited

Some non-EU licences may be valid only temporarily after becoming resident.

Exchange agreements

Some countries have licence-exchange agreements with Spain, but the exact rules may vary by country of issue.

Testing may be required

If no exchange agreement applies, a Spanish driving test may be required.

International Driving Permit

An International Driving Permit may help temporarily, but it does not replace residency rules.

Licence exchange

What exchange means

Licence exchange means replacing your foreign licence with a Spanish driving licence.

Requirements vary

Requirements may vary depending on the issuing country and the category of licence.

Common items

You may need an appointment, identity documents, residence documents, photo, medical report and official fee.

Check with DGT

DGT is the authority to check before you book, pay or submit documents.

Important

Do not assume your country has an exchange agreement. Check the current DGT rules for your specific licence before making plans.

Renewal in Spain

Resident renewal

If you are resident in Spain and your licence expires, you may need to renew through Spain.

Medical check

Renewal usually involves a medical check at an authorised centre.

Licence category and age

Rules may depend on licence category, age and other circumstances.

Expiry dates

Keep track of your expiry date so you are not forced to resolve the issue urgently.

Driving with a foreign car

Separate from the licence

Car registration rules are separate from driving-licence rules.

Insurance matters

You should confirm that your insurance is valid for your situation and where the car is being used.

ITV may apply

Spanish vehicle-inspection rules may apply depending on the vehicle and registration status.

Complex procedures

Import and registration procedures can be complex, especially after becoming resident.

Keep the topics separate

Licence exchange, vehicle registration, insurance and ITV are connected in everyday driving, but they are different administrative questions.

Real Questions People Ask

Can I drive in Spain with an EU licence?

Usually yes while the licence is valid, but resident renewal and medical rules may apply. Check DGT guidance for your exact licence.

Do I need to exchange my Finnish licence?

A Finnish licence is an EU licence, so it is generally recognised while valid. If it expires while you are resident in Spain, renewal may need to happen in Spain.

Can I drive with a non-EU licence?

Possibly for a limited period, depending on the country of issue, your residency status and whether an exchange agreement applies.

What is the DGT?

DGT is Spain’s traffic authority and the official place to check driving-licence, exchange and renewal rules.

Do I need a medical check?

You may need one for renewal or exchange. Requirements can depend on your licence category, age and procedure.

Is car registration the same as licence exchange?

No. Licence exchange is about your right to drive. Car registration is about the vehicle.

What happens if my licence expires while I live in Spain?

You may need to renew it in Spain if you are resident. Check the current DGT process before it expires.
