Taxes When Living in Spain

Tax is one of the areas where early planning matters. This guide explains the first concepts to understand before you rely on assumptions from your home country.

Get advice early

Small tax decisions can become expensive later. Use this page as orientation, not as personal tax advice.

Quick Answer

If you live in Spain, you need to understand tax residence, tax address, annual income tax, foreign income and reporting obligations. Tax residence is not the same as immigration residence, and the answer can depend on days in Spain, where your main interests are, family ties and treaty rules.

At a Glance

Other indicatorsEconomic interests, family and personal centre can also matter.

Before You Start

Separate immigration from tax

Having EU registration or a visa does not automatically answer every tax-residence question.

Map your income

List salary, pensions, dividends, rent, business income, capital gains and foreign accounts.

Track days

Keep a simple record of time spent in Spain and other countries.

Important

Do not wait until the filing season if you have income outside Spain, remote work, a company, property, investments or complex family ties.

Who Needs This?

People moving long term

Use this if Spain may become your normal home or main base.

Remote workers and self-employed people

Tax and Social Security questions can be more complex when income crosses borders.

Retirees and people with assets

Pensions, investments, property and foreign accounts can create reporting questions.

Official Requirements

Tax residence

Spain looks at factors such as days in Spain, economic interests and personal/family centre. Tax treaties can also matter.

IRPF

Spanish resident income tax is commonly known as IRPF, or declaración de la renta when filing the annual return.

Tax address

Your domicilio fiscal is the tax address recorded with the Tax Agency and should be kept current.

Important distinction

This guide explains the starting concepts. It does not calculate your tax residence or tax due.

Practical Advice

Organize evidence

Keep travel records, work contracts, payslips, pension statements, bank statements and rental contracts.

Check before invoicing

If you plan to freelance or invoice clients, get advice before you start billing.

Review double-tax issues

Foreign income can interact with tax treaties, home-country filing and Spanish filing.

Tip

Create a simple tax folder before you need it: identity documents, NIE, address proof, bank details, income records and foreign tax documents.

Tax address and notifications

Domicilio fiscal

This is the address the Tax Agency uses for tax purposes. It may need updating after you settle.

Digital access

A digital certificate or Cl@ve can help you review data, certificates and some procedures online.

Notices

Once you use online services, pay attention to electronic notices and official messages.

Important

Ignoring tax notices can create problems even if you did not understand the online system. Set up access carefully and check it periodically.

Common Mistakes

1

Assuming immigration residence and tax residence are the same thing.

2

Counting only salary and forgetting pensions, rent, investments or foreign income.

3

Waiting until tax season to ask for advice.

4

Ignoring the tax address recorded with the Tax Agency.

5

Assuming a home-country accountant understands Spanish residence rules.

Real Questions People Ask

Does spending more than 183 days in Spain make me tax resident?

It is a major indicator, but not the only factor. Economic interests, family centre and treaty rules can also matter.

Do I pay tax only on Spanish income?

If you are Spanish tax resident, worldwide income can become relevant. Get advice if you have income or assets abroad.

What is domicilio fiscal?

It is your tax address recorded with the Tax Agency. It should match your real tax situation.

Should I use a gestor or tax adviser?

If you have foreign income, self-employment, a company, assets or uncertainty about residence, professional advice is sensible.
