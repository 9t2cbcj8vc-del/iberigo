A complete step-by-step roadmap for anyone planning to move to Spain.

1

Can I afford Spain?

Before choosing forms, work out the practical money picture: rent, deposits, health cover, transport, banking, taxes and the first-month buffer. The paperwork route is much easier when the budget is honest.

🏦BankingOpen and use a Spanish account, or start with a temporary option. ⌁TaxesUnderstand tax address, residency and the Agencia Tributaria basics. ⌂Renting a homePrepare for deposits, documents, contract types and red flags. ◌Non-Lucrative VisaFor non-EU applicants living in Spain without working.
2

Can I find work?

Your work rights depend on nationality and permit route. EU citizens can work freely, while many non-EU routes need authorization before work starts. Remote work, Spanish employment and self-employment are not the same path.

⌕Job searchPublic and private portals for finding work in Spain. ◆Social SecurityGet your number for employment, autónomo activity and contributions. ◈Digital NomadRemote work mainly for clients or companies outside Spain. ↗Work authorizationFor non-EU work and residence routes tied to Spanish work.
3

How does healthcare work?

Healthcare depends on your route. Some people need private insurance for a visa or EU registration, workers may enter the public system through Social Security, and short visitors should not treat travel cover as a long-term plan.

＋Public healthcare cardRegional cards such as SIP, TSI or Tarjeta Sanitaria Individual after you qualify. ◎Private health insuranceOften important for non-working EU residents, non-lucrative visas and some consular routes. ◆Social SecurityWork or self-employment can connect you to public healthcare through the Spanish system. ✚EHICUseful for temporary EU trips, but not a substitute for settled healthcare in Spain.
4

What is everyday life like?

Moving is not only a visa question. Daily life means an address, phone number, banking, digital access, driving rules and local appointments that often depend on each other.

⌂PadrónTown hall address registration, often needed for later steps. ⌘Digital CertificateFNMT or Cl@ve access for Spanish public services online. ◍Phone and internetGet a Spanish number for banking, appointments and daily setup. ☷Driving LicenceWhen and how to exchange a foreign licence after moving.
5

Can I travel to Spain and apply later?

That depends mostly on nationality and the type of stay. EU/EEA/Swiss citizens can usually move first and register after arrival. Many non-EU routes must be started from outside Spain before you treat Spain as your home.

★EU RegistrationFor EU, EEA and Swiss citizens living in Spain longer term. ◈Digital NomadInternational telework route for eligible non-EU applicants. ◌Non-Lucrative VisaResidence without working in Spain. ♡Family ReunificationJoining family through standard or EU-family routes.
6

Where do I actually start?

Start with status, purpose and timing. Then prepare identity documents, address proof, money evidence, health cover and the forms that match the route. Avoid booking random appointments before knowing the procedure label.

#NIEForeigner identification number for many Spanish procedures. ▤TIEPhysical identity card after non-EU residence approval. ⌂PadrónRegister your Spanish address with the town hall. ★EU RegistrationGreen certificate route for EU/EEA/Swiss residents.
7

Where can I get help?

IberiGo is a starting point, not a professional adviser. Use the guides to understand the route, then check official sources or speak with a qualified professional when the decision affects residence, taxes, work rights or family status.

⌘Digital CertificateAccess official portals and track public administration steps. ⌁TaxesKnow when tax residency and official address details matter. ◆Social SecurityWork, contributions and public-system records. ♡Family ReunificationEU-family and non-EU family routes can differ sharply.

Your next step

Pick the route that sounds closest to your situation. You do not need to solve everything today; the aim is to choose the first reliable path and avoid starting with the wrong appointment.

★EU/EEA/Swiss: start with EU registration ◌Non-EU: choose the right visa route first ⌂Already arriving: prepare address and padrón steps
