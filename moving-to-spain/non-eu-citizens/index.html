Quick Answer

Non-EU citizens usually need a visa, residence authorisation or another recognised legal route to live in Spain. The correct process depends on why you are moving. Common routes include work, study, family, retirement or sufficient resources, digital nomad status, self-employment, and joining an EU citizen family member. Many people eventually need a TIE after approval. This roadmap helps you identify the right next guide — it does not replace legal advice.

TIE basics

What TIE is

TIE (Tarjeta de Identidad de Extranjero) is the physical foreigner identity card used by many non-EU residents.

Not the same as NIE

NIE is an identification number. TIE is a physical card. They are not the same thing.

Not the same as EU Registration

EU Registration is the certificate route used by EU, EEA and Swiss citizens. Non-EU residents normally follow the TIE route instead.

Fingerprinting and collection

Many non-EU residents go through a fingerprinting and card-collection process after approval.

Details depend on your route

Exact steps, timing and required documents depend on your specific residence route.

Real Questions People Ask

Can I move to Spain without being an EU citizen?

Yes, but you usually need a visa, residence authorisation or another recognised legal route. The correct process depends on your situation and nationality.

Do non-EU citizens need a visa?

Often yes, depending on the route and the length and purpose of stay. Some routes may involve a visa before travel; others may differ. Check the official requirements for your case.

Is NIE the same as TIE?

No. NIE is an identification number. TIE is a physical foreigner identity card. Many non-EU residents end up with both, but they are not the same document.

Do I need padrón before TIE?

It depends on the office and route. Padrón may be requested as part of some procedures. Check the requirements for your specific appointment.

Can I work in Spain as a non-EU citizen?

It depends on your residence route and any work authorisation attached to it. Some routes allow work; others may restrict it or require a separate step.

Can I study in Spain?

Study routes exist for non-EU citizens, but requirements depend on the programme, duration and your nationality.

Can I join my EU spouse or partner in Spain?

Family member of an EU citizen is a distinct route that can differ from standard non-EU family routes. Check the specific requirements that apply to your relationship and situation.
