Healthcare in Spain for EU Citizens

Healthcare in Spain depends on why you are moving. This guide separates the main EU citizen situations so you can see which route may apply before EU registration or daily-life setup.

No single answer

A worker, retiree, student and self-sufficient person may need different healthcare evidence. Do not assume everyone needs private insurance.

Quick Answer

Your healthcare route depends on why you are moving to Spain. If you work or are self-employed, public healthcare may connect to Social Security. If you are retired, an S1 certificate may apply. If you study or live from savings, you may need public entitlement or comprehensive private cover, especially when proving healthcare for EU registration.

Before You Start

Name your route

Decide whether your likely route is work, self-employment, S1, student cover, private insurance or another public entitlement.

Check timing

Healthcare proof may be needed before EU registration, especially if you are not working.

Separate temporary and resident cover

EHIC and travel cover are not the same as planning healthcare as a resident.

Tip

If you are preparing EU registration, read this page before booking or attending the EU Registration Certificate appointment.

Official Requirements

Evidence follows your situation

EU registration and healthcare access can depend on whether you work, are self-employed, study, receive an S1 or live from savings.

Public entitlement must be recognized

Public healthcare usually starts with a recognized entitlement before the regional health service issues a card.

Private cover may need to be comprehensive

If private insurance is your evidence route, check that the policy is suitable for living in Spain and for the process using it.

Important

Do not treat travel insurance or an EHIC as automatic proof for living in Spain. Check the evidence required for your route.

Practical Advice

Start with your reason for moving

The right healthcare route is usually easier to identify once you know whether work, self-employment, retirement, study or savings applies.

Ask for written proof

Keep certificates, policy summaries, Social Security evidence and regional health-service confirmations.

Plan for local variation

The health card name, appointment process and documents can vary by autonomous community.

Working in Spain

Official requirement

If you are working as an employee, your employer-related Social Security registration is usually the starting point for public healthcare entitlement.

Practical advice

Confirm that your employer has handled the correct work registration before relying on healthcare access.

What to keep

Save your Social Security number, work registration evidence and any healthcare entitlement confirmation.

Tip

Ask your employer who handles Social Security registration and when you can request or activate your regional health card.

Self-employed

Official requirement

Self-employed EU citizens usually need to look at autónomo registration and Social Security contribution as the starting point.

Practical advice

Do not separate healthcare planning from your tax and autónomo setup. They often move together.

What to keep

Save autónomo registration, Social Security documents and payment confirmations.

Important

Self-employment setup has tax and Social Security consequences. Get qualified help if you are unsure.

Retired

Official requirement

Many EU retirees start by checking whether their competent country can issue an S1 certificate for healthcare in Spain.

Practical advice

Request the S1 early. It can take time, and you may need it before completing later steps in Spain.

What to keep

Keep the S1 certificate, identity documents, address evidence and any Spanish health service registration proof.

Tip

If the S1 route applies to you, keep copies of the certificate before handing documents to any office.

Student

Official requirement

Students should check whether they have public healthcare entitlement, accepted EU coverage, or need comprehensive insurance.

Practical advice

Ask your school or university what proof they expect, but verify official requirements separately.

What to keep

Keep enrolment proof, insurance documents, public entitlement evidence and identity documents together.

Editorial note

Student healthcare evidence can depend on the programme, length of stay and personal situation.

Living from savings

Official requirement

EU citizens who are not working may need to show sufficient healthcare cover when registering as residents.

Practical advice

Do not assume travel insurance is enough for living in Spain. Check whether the cover is comprehensive and accepted for your process.

What to keep

Keep policy documents, coverage summaries, payment proof and any official entitlement evidence.

Important

This is the situation where people often wrongly assume private insurance is optional or that travel insurance is enough. Verify before the EU registration appointment.

Private health insurance

When it may be relevant

Private insurance may be relevant if you are not covered through work, self-employment, an S1 or another public entitlement.

What to check

Check whether the policy is comprehensive, valid in Spain, and suitable for the process you are using it for.

What not to assume

Do not assume every policy, travel policy, excess or limited cover will be accepted.

Tip

Ask the insurer for a clear certificate or policy summary in a format you can show during admin steps.

Public healthcare

What it means

Public healthcare access generally starts with a recognized right to healthcare, then registration with the regional health service.

Common starting points

Work registration, self-employment registration, recognized dependent status, or an S1-type route may be relevant depending on your situation.

Regional card

Once your right is recognized, the regional health service issues the local health card.

Important

Spain’s public healthcare system is national in structure but administered through regional health services. The card name and steps can vary by autonomous community.

S1 certificate

What it is for

An S1 certificate can allow certain people, often pensioners or posted workers, to register healthcare cover in another EU country.

Who issues it

The competent country normally issues the S1, not the Spanish health centre.

What to do next

Once issued, it is normally used in Spain to register healthcare entitlement through the relevant process.

Check your country

Whether you can get an S1 depends on your personal situation and competent country. Check with the institution responsible for your healthcare cover.

How to get a SIP card

What SIP means

SIP is the common name for the health card in the Valencian Community. Other regions use names such as tarjeta sanitaria individual or their own regional wording.

Step 1

First, confirm your right to healthcare through work, self-employment, S1, public entitlement or accepted cover.

Step 2

Then follow your regional health service process to register and request the health card.

Documents often useful for a regional health card

  • Identity document.
  • NIE or residence/registration details if you have them.
  • Padrón or local address evidence if requested.
  • Proof of healthcare entitlement, such as Social Security recognition or S1 registration.
  • Contact details and regional application form if required.
Important

SIP is region-specific wording. Do not assume the same card name or exact process applies outside the Valencian Community.

Real Questions People Ask

Do all EU citizens moving to Spain need private health insurance?

No. It depends on why you are moving and whether you have public healthcare entitlement through work, self-employment, S1 or another accepted route.

Do I need healthcare proof for EU registration?

It depends on your circumstances. Non-working or self-sufficient applicants often need to show healthcare cover, while workers may use work-related evidence.

Is EHIC enough if I move to Spain?

EHIC is for temporary stays. Moving to Spain normally requires thinking about resident healthcare access, not only travel cover.

What is the difference between SIP and a health card?

SIP is the Valencian Community health card name. Other regions use different wording for their regional health card.

Can I get public healthcare if I work in Spain?

Work registration and Social Security are usually the starting point, but you should confirm your registration and health entitlement.

Should I arrange healthcare before EU registration?

Yes, if your EU registration route may require healthcare evidence. Do not wait until the appointment to discover what proof you need.

What Happens Next?

Once you understand your healthcare route, prepare the proof that matches your situation. Then continue with EU registration, banking, digital access and tax planning.
