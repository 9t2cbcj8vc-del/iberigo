Healthcare in Spain for New Residents

A practical guide to understanding public healthcare, private insurance, S1 forms and regional health cards before and after moving to Spain.

6 min read

No single answer

Healthcare access can depend on work status, Social Security, pension rights, S1 entitlement, private insurance, region, residence route and family status.

Quick Answer

Spain has a public healthcare system and private healthcare options. Access to public healthcare depends on your situation. Workers are often connected through Social Security. Some pensioners may use an S1 form. Some residents need private health insurance, especially for certain immigration or self-sufficient routes. Healthcare rules and regional health card procedures can vary by autonomous community.

Public healthcare in Spain

Regional management

Public healthcare is managed through regional health services in each autonomous community.

Eligibility routes

Eligibility may come through employment, Social Security, pension rights, family status or other recognised routes.

Separate documents

The regional health card is not the same as the NIE, padrón or Social Security number.

Regional procedures

Health-card procedures and document requests can vary by autonomous community.

Important

Do not assume public healthcare is automatic just because you live in Spain or have a padrón. Your recognised healthcare route still matters.

Private health insurance

May be useful or required

Private insurance may be useful or required depending on your residency route and whether you have a recognised public healthcare route.

Comprehensive cover

Some immigration or residence procedures may require comprehensive health cover.

Policy details

Coverage, exclusions, waiting periods and copayments can vary by policy.

Check the exact requirement

Before buying, check whether the policy meets the requirement for your specific procedure.

Important

Do not choose an insurer based only on price. Check the coverage, exclusions and whether the policy is suitable for the procedure using it.

S1 form

Who it may help

Some pensioners or people covered by another EU/EEA country may be able to use an S1 form.

What it does

It can help register healthcare entitlement in Spain.

Eligibility

Eligibility depends on the issuing country and your personal situation.

Where to check

Check with the authority responsible for healthcare cover in your home or competent country.

S1 is not the same as EHIC

An S1 is used for certain residence-linked healthcare entitlement situations. EHIC is generally for temporary stays.

Regional health card

Issued regionally

The health card is usually issued by the regional health service in your autonomous community.

Requirements vary

Documents and steps can vary by autonomous community.

Common documents

You may need padrón, identity documents, Social Security recognition, S1 registration or other proof.

Separate from residency

The health card is separate from immigration residency, NIE, padrón and Social Security number.

Documents you may need

  • Passport, national ID or residence document
  • NIE, if available
  • Padrón certificate, where required
  • Social Security recognition or registration, if applicable
  • S1 registration, if applicable
  • Regional health service form, if required
Tip

Check with your regional health service before the appointment so you know which documents they expect.

Healthcare before EU registration

Why timing matters

Some EU citizens may need to show healthcare cover before or during EU Registration Certificate steps.

Self-sufficient, retired or students

People applying as self-sufficient, retired or students may need to prepare healthcare evidence before the appointment.

Workers

Workers may use work-related Social Security evidence, depending on the appointment requirements.

Check before booking

Review the exact appointment instructions before assuming which healthcare proof will be accepted.

Important

Healthcare planning may need to happen before the EU registration appointment, especially if your route is not based on employment in Spain.

Real Questions People Ask

Do I automatically get public healthcare if I move to Spain?

No. Public healthcare access depends on your recognised route, such as work, Social Security, pension rights, family status or another entitlement.

Is private health insurance required?

Sometimes. It depends on your residency route and whether you have another accepted healthcare route.

Is padrón enough to get healthcare?

Usually not by itself. Padrón may be part of local registration, but healthcare entitlement depends on your situation.

What is an S1 form?

An S1 is a form that may allow certain people, often pensioners or people covered by another EU/EEA country, to register healthcare entitlement in Spain.

Can I use my EHIC after moving?

EHIC is generally for temporary stays. If you move to Spain, you should plan resident healthcare access rather than relying only on tourist cover.

Do I need healthcare before EU registration?

You may, especially if you are applying as self-sufficient, retired or a student. Check your appointment instructions.

Is the health card the same as Social Security?

No. A regional health card, Social Security number, NIE and padrón are separate documents or records, even when they interact.

Does healthcare work the same in every region?

No. Public healthcare is managed through regional health services, so procedures and health-card requirements can vary.
