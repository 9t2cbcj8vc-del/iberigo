Quick Answer

Students from outside Spain may need a recognised study route, visa or authorisation depending on nationality and length of stay. Requirements can depend on course type, duration, nationality and where the application is made. Non-EU students may eventually need a TIE if staying longer. EU students follow a different process and may need EU Registration if staying longer than three months. This roadmap helps you understand the general journey — it does not replace official requirements.

Real Questions People Ask

Do I need a visa to study in Spain?

It depends on your nationality, course type and study length. Some students need a visa or study authorisation; others may not. Check the official requirements for your case.

Can EU citizens study in Spain without a visa?

Usually yes. EU, EEA and Swiss students do not usually need a visa, though EU registration may apply if staying longer than three months.

Do students need a TIE?

Many non-EU students staying longer may need a TIE. Requirements depend on your route and approval.

Can students work in Spain?

It depends on the student's status, route and current rules. Do not assume work rights are automatic — verify before accepting work.

Is NIE the same as TIE?

No. NIE is an identification number. TIE is a physical foreigner identity card. They are not the same document.

Do I need private health insurance?

It depends on your route and nationality. Some students may need private insurance; others may use another healthcare route.

Can I use temporary accommodation?

It may work for some initial needs, but temporary accommodation may not always support every administrative step, such as padrón. Check before relying on it.
