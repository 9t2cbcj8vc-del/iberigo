Quick Answer

People moving to Spain for work may follow different routes depending on nationality and job situation. EU citizens and non-EU citizens follow different processes. Non-EU citizens usually need the correct work and residence authorisation before working. Employees, highly qualified workers, seasonal workers and remote workers may follow different routes. This roadmap helps you understand the general journey — it does not replace official requirements.

Real Questions People Ask

Can I move to Spain for work?

Often yes, but the correct route depends on your nationality, job offer and situation. Check the official requirements that apply to your case.

Can EU citizens work in Spain without a visa?

Usually yes. EU, EEA and Swiss citizens do not usually need a visa to work, though EU Registration may apply if staying longer than three months.

Can non-EU citizens work in Spain with only a job offer?

Not by itself. A job offer usually needs to be supported by the correct work and residence authorisation before working legally.

Is NIE the same as permission to work?

No. NIE is an identification number. Permission to work depends on your residence and work authorisation, which is separate.

Do workers need a TIE?

Many non-EU workers may need a TIE after approval or arrival. Requirements depend on your route.

Does my employer register me with Social Security?

Employers usually handle this once the employment relationship is valid, but you should ask for confirmation and keep your own records.

Do I need healthcare before working?

It depends on your route and situation. Some healthcare evidence may be requested as part of certain procedures.

Will working in Spain affect my taxes?

It can. Working in Spain may create Spanish tax obligations, and tax residency is a separate question from immigration residency. Review your tax position early.
