Finding Accommodation in Spain

A practical guide to finding a place to live in Spain, understanding rental basics, preparing documents, and avoiding common problems before you sign.

7 min read

Your address affects later steps

A rental may affect padrón registration, healthcare, banking, official notifications and local services, so check address paperwork before you commit.

Quick Answer

Many newcomers start with temporary accommodation before signing a long-term rental. Long-term rentals may require documents and proof of income. The padrón may depend on having an address where registration is possible. Review rental contracts carefully before signing and consider professional advice if anything is unclear. Scams and misleading listings are possible, especially online.

Temporary vs long-term accommodation

Temporary: useful when arriving

Temporary accommodation can help you arrive calmly while you learn the area and view homes in person.

Temporary: easier before documents

It may be easier to book before you have Spanish documents or proof of local income.

Temporary: padrón may not be possible

Short-term accommodation may not always provide the documents needed for padrón registration.

Long-term: more stability

A long-term rental is usually better for stable living, local services and daily administration.

Long-term: more documents

Long-term rentals may require more documents and stronger proof of income or guarantees.

Long-term: contract terms matter

Contract duration, notice rules, deposits, utilities and address paperwork should be checked before signing.

Ask before you sign

Do not assume every rental allows padrón. Ask in writing whether the landlord or agency can provide the documents needed for municipal registration.

Why the address matters

Padrón registration

Your address may affect whether and where you can register on the padrón.

Healthcare registration

Local healthcare registration may depend on where you live and your regional health service.

School registration

For families, school access or catchment questions may depend on address.

Bank paperwork

Banks may ask for address information or proof depending on the account and your status.

Official notifications

Your address may be used for official correspondence and administrative records.

Local services

Transport, health centres, town hall offices and daily services vary by neighbourhood and municipality.

Important

Before signing, ask whether the landlord will provide the documents needed for padrón registration. Get important confirmations in writing.

Documents landlords may ask for

Identity document

A passport or national ID is commonly requested.

NIE, if available

Some landlords or agencies may ask for a NIE if you already have one.

Work and income

An employment contract, payslips, proof of income or bank references may be requested.

Rental history

Previous rental references may help, especially in competitive areas.

Deposit

A deposit and upfront payment are commonly requested before move-in.

Guarantee

A guarantor or additional guarantee may be requested in some cases.

Tip

Exact requirements vary by landlord and agency. Ask for the required document list before arranging a viewing or sending sensitive information.

Rental contracts

Read carefully

Read the contract carefully and consider professional advice before signing if anything is unclear.

Core terms

Check rent, deposit, duration, renewal, notice rules and move-in date.

Utilities

Check which utilities are included and which may need to be contracted or paid separately.

Extra charges

Ask who pays community fees, rubbish tax or other recurring charges where applicable.

Padrón documents

Check whether the property can be used for padrón and what documents the landlord will provide.

Keep proof

Keep signed copies, receipts, bank-transfer records and written confirmations.

Deposits and upfront payments

Deposit and guarantees

Landlords may ask for a deposit and additional guarantees, depending on the rental and your situation.

Agency fees

Agency fees may apply in some situations, so ask what each payment is for before agreeing.

Verify before paying

Avoid sending large payments without verifying the property, contract and recipient.

Traceable payments

Use traceable payment methods and avoid unclear payment routes.

Receipts

Keep receipts and written confirmation for every payment.

Important

If you feel pressured to pay before you can verify the listing or contract, slow down and check the details carefully.

Avoiding scams

Too good to be true

A very low price in a high-demand area can be a warning sign.

No viewing or video call

Be cautious if the landlord refuses a viewing, video call or reasonable verification.

Pressure to pay quickly

Scammers often create urgency so you do not check the details.

Poor or stolen photos

Photos that look inconsistent, generic or reused can be a warning sign.

No contract

Avoid paying large sums without a clear contract or written terms.

Suspicious payment details

Check whether the payment recipient and account details make sense for the property and agreement.

Sensitive documents too early

Be cautious if identity documents are requested unusually early without context.

Details do not match

Verify that address, photos, viewing details, landlord or agency information and contract details match.

Real Questions People Ask

Can I use Airbnb or temporary accommodation for padrón?

Sometimes it may be possible, but do not assume it. Ask whether the accommodation can provide the documents your town hall requires.

Do I need a rental contract for padrón?

Town halls usually ask for some form of address evidence. The exact documents can vary by municipality and situation.

Can I rent before getting a NIE?

It may be possible, depending on the landlord or agency. Some may accept a passport or national ID, while others may ask for a NIE.

What documents do landlords ask for?

They may ask for identity documents, NIE if available, proof of income, work contract, payslips, references, deposit and sometimes extra guarantees.

Should I pay before viewing?

Be very cautious. If you cannot view in person, try to verify through a video call, agency checks, contract review and traceable payments before sending money.

Can a landlord refuse padrón registration?

The practical issue is often whether the landlord will provide the documents needed by the town hall. Ask before signing and keep written confirmation.

Is it better to rent short-term first?

Many newcomers do because it gives time to compare areas and avoid rushing. It may not solve padrón or long-term paperwork needs, so plan the next step early.
