Moving to Spain as an EU Citizen

Planning to move to Spain as an EU citizen? This guide takes you through the process step by step, from preparing your move to settling into everyday life in Spain.

Follow the journey, not the paperwork. We start with your situation, then move through arrival, registration, healthcare, banking, tax and daily life setup.

Quick Facts

Visa required?No.
Staying longer than 3 months?EU registration required.
Can you work?Yes.
TIE card?Normally no. EU citizens receive a Certificate of Registration.
DifficultyModerate.
Typical timelineA few weeks after arrival, depending on appointments.

6Before registration

Arrange your healthcare

Why this matters

Healthcare comes before EU registration for many non-working or self-sufficient EU citizens because proof of cover may be needed.

Practical advice

Do not rely on ordinary travel insurance if you are moving to live in Spain.

Next step

Confirm whether your cover comes through work, public entitlement, or private insurance.

What you’ll usually need

  • Public healthcare evidence, or
  • Private health insurance
  • Policy documents if using private cover

7After documents ready

Register as an EU citizen

Why this matters

This is the main residence registration step for EU, EEA and Swiss citizens staying in Spain longer term.

Practical advice

Use the appointment and fee that match EU citizen registration, not a TIE card route.

Next step

Prepare your EX-18 route evidence and book the correct appointment.

Important distinction

EU citizens normally apply for the EU Registration Certificate, not a TIE. TIE cards are generally for non-EU nationals.

What you’ll usually need

  • Identity document
  • EX-18
  • Padrón if requested
  • Work, funds, study or healthcare evidence
  • Modelo 790-012 payment

Use the EU registration guide for forms, fees and appointment wording.
