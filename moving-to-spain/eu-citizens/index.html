Moving to Spain as an EU Citizen

Planning to move to Spain as an EU citizen? This roadmap shows the usual order of decisions and admin steps, from preparation to settling into daily life.

Follow the journey

Start with your situation, then move through address, healthcare, registration, banking, tax and driving checks.

How to use this guide

This guide gives you the big picture. Each step points to a more detailed IberiGo guide with documents, requirements, costs and practical advice.

Phase 1 – Planning

  1. 1

    Confirm your route

    Your evidence depends on whether you are working, self-employed, studying, retired, or living from savings.

  2. 2

    Prepare your documents

    Make a small folder for identity, work or funds, healthcare and address documents.

  3. 3

    Plan your move

    Plan the first month around address proof and appointments, not only travel logistics.

Phase 2 – Arrival

  1. 1

    Secure accommodation

    Your address affects padrón, banking, healthcare and many local admin steps.

  2. 2

    Register on the padrón

    The padrón is town hall address registration and often acts as local address evidence.

  3. 3

    Arrange your healthcare

    Healthcare comes before EU registration for many non-working or self-sufficient EU citizens because proof of cover may be needed.

  4. 4

    Register as an EU citizen

    Prepare your EX-18 route evidence and book the correct EU registration appointment.

Important

EU citizens normally apply for the EU Registration Certificate, not a TIE. TIE cards are generally for non-EU nationals.

Phase 3 – Settling In

  1. 1

    Register with Social Security, if applicable

    Separate getting a Social Security number from being registered as active for work.

  2. 2

    Open a bank account

    Compare documents, fees and account options before choosing.

  3. 3

    Get a Digital Certificate or Cl@ve

    Digital access helps with tax, Social Security and many public services.

  4. 4

    Understand your tax obligations

    Review tax risk early, especially if you have income or assets outside Spain.

  5. 5

    Check your driving licence

    Driving rules depend on where your licence was issued and whether you become resident.

You're Ready

You’ve completed the main steps most EU citizens follow when moving to Spain. From here, IberiGo’s detailed guides can help with everyday life, healthcare, banking, taxes and local administration.
