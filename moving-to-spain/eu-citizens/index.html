A practical roadmap for EU, EEA and Swiss citizens moving to Spain. Follow the steps in this general order, then use the detailed guides when a step needs documents or local checks.

Quick Answer

EU, EEA and Swiss citizens can move to Spain without a visa. If you are staying longer than three months, you normally need to register as an EU resident. The main journey usually includes accommodation, padrón, healthcare, EU registration and everyday setup. The exact order and requirements can vary depending on your situation and location.

Use this as a roadmap

The order matters more than the speed

In some municipalities or provinces, appointments may be available quickly. In others, waiting times can be much longer. This roadmap helps you understand what to prepare and what usually comes next.

Real Questions People Ask

Can EU citizens move to Spain without a visa?

Yes, EU, EEA and Swiss citizens can usually enter Spain without a visa. Longer stays still involve local administration and EU residence registration.

Do EU citizens need a TIE?

Normally no. EU citizens usually receive a green EU Registration Certificate. TIE cards are generally used by non-EU citizens.

Is NIE the same as EU registration?

No. NIE is an identification number. EU registration is the residence registration process for EU citizens staying longer than three months.

Do I need padrón before EU registration?

It may be requested locally and can be useful for other steps. Check the requirements for your appointment and municipality.

Do I need healthcare before registering?

It depends on your route. Students, retirees and self-sufficient applicants may need healthcare proof before EU registration.

Can I work before EU registration?

EU citizens can generally work in Spain, but employment, Social Security and residence registration steps still need to be handled correctly.

What if appointments are not available?

Appointment availability can vary by province. Keep preparing documents, check official channels and avoid booking the wrong process out of frustration.

What should I do first after arriving?

Secure accommodation and understand whether the address can support padrón registration. From there, arrange padrón, healthcare and EU registration according to your situation.

Official Sources

Spanish Government

Punto de Acceso General — the central official entry point for Spanish public administration procedures.

Ministry responsible for immigration

Ministerio de Inclusión, Seguridad Social y Migraciones — Migraciones section, responsible for EU registration and residence procedures.

Police appointment portal

Policía Nacional e-office, where appointment booking for foreigners' procedures is available. Check the current appointment options for your specific procedure.

Local Town Halls

There is no single official website — padrón and local address requirements are set by each municipality. Check your own town hall's website for local instructions.
