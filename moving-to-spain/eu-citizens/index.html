Quick Answer

EU citizens can move to Spain without a visa, but living in Spain longer term still involves practical admin: housing, padrón, healthcare, EU registration, banking, digital access, tax review and driving checks. The right order depends on your route and local appointment availability.

At a Glance

Visa required?No.
Staying longer than 3 months?EU registration required.
Can you work?Yes.
TIE card?Normally no. EU citizens receive a Certificate of Registration.
DifficultyModerate.
Typical timelineA few weeks after arrival, depending on appointments.

What you need before you start

A clear reason for moving

Work, self-employment, study, retirement or savings affects later evidence.

A realistic address plan

Your first home can affect padrón, healthcare, banking and other admin steps.

A document folder

Start with identity, address, healthcare, work or funds evidence, and appointment confirmations.

Official Requirements

No visa for EU citizens

EU, EEA and Swiss citizens do not need a visa to enter Spain for this route.

Longer-term stay

If you live in Spain longer term, you usually need the EU Registration Certificate rather than a TIE card.

Your situation controls the proof

The documents you prepare depend on whether you work, study, retire, run a business or live from savings.

Important

This roadmap explains the usual sequence. Always check the official appointment instructions for the specific process you are about to complete.

Practical Advice

Do not wait for perfect order

Appointments may not line up neatly. Prepare documents in parallel so one delay does not block every next step.

Treat address proof as a foundation

Housing and padrón often affect healthcare, banking, school and registration steps.

Keep one Spain folder

Save identity, address, healthcare, registration, banking and tax documents in one place from the first week.

Step 2: Handle arrival admin

  1. 1

    Secure accommodation

    Your address affects padrón, banking, healthcare and many local admin steps.

  2. 2

    Register on the padrón

    The padrón is town hall address registration and often acts as local address evidence.

  3. 3

    Arrange your healthcare

    Healthcare comes before EU registration for many non-working or self-sufficient EU citizens because proof of cover may be needed.

  4. 4

    Register as an EU citizen

    Prepare your EX-18 route evidence and book the correct EU registration appointment.

Important

EU citizens normally apply for the EU Registration Certificate, not a TIE. TIE cards are generally for non-EU nationals.

Step 3: Settle into daily life

  1. 1

    Register with Social Security, if applicable

    Separate getting a Social Security number from being registered as active for work.

  2. 2

    Open a bank account

    Compare documents, fees and account options before choosing.

  3. 3

    Get a Digital Certificate or Cl@ve

    Digital access helps with tax, Social Security and many public services.

  4. 4

    Understand your tax obligations

    Review tax risk early, especially if you have income or assets outside Spain.

  5. 5

    Check your driving licence

    Driving rules depend on where your licence was issued and whether you become resident.

Common Mistakes

1

Assuming EU freedom of movement means there is no local administration.

2

Booking a TIE appointment when the EU Registration Certificate is the relevant route.

3

Leaving healthcare evidence until the EU registration appointment.

4

Signing accommodation without checking whether it can support padrón registration.

5

Ignoring tax and driving questions until they become urgent.

Real Questions People Ask

Do EU citizens need a visa to move to Spain?

No. EU citizens do not need a visa for this route, but longer-term residence registration and local admin can still apply.

Should I get the padrón before EU registration?

It is often useful and may be requested. Check your appointment instructions and prepare address evidence early.

Does every EU citizen need private health insurance?

No. Healthcare evidence depends on whether you work, are self-employed, retired, studying or living from savings.

Is the TIE for EU citizens?

Usually no. EU citizens normally receive a Certificate of Registration. TIE cards are generally for non-EU nationals.
