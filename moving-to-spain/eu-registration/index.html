EU Registration in Spain

A practical guide to registering as an EU, EEA or Swiss citizen in Spain, preparing the right evidence, and avoiding confusion with NIE, TIE and padrón.

6 min read

Not a TIE

Quick Answer

EU, EEA and Swiss citizens can enter Spain without a visa. If you are staying longer than three months, you normally need to register as an EU resident. The result is usually the EU Registration Certificate, which includes a NIE. It is not the same as a TIE.

At a Glance

Who needs EU registration?

EU citizens staying longer than three months

If you live in Spain beyond a short stay, EU registration is normally the residence registration route to understand.

EEA citizens

EEA citizens usually follow the same general registration route.

Swiss citizens

Swiss citizens are commonly included in this registration framework.

Workers

Employees may need work evidence and related registration documents.

Self-employed people

Self-employed applicants may need evidence of activity, tax or Social Security setup.

Students

Students may need study evidence, healthcare proof and resources evidence depending on the appointment.

Retirees

Retirees may need pension or sufficient resources evidence and healthcare proof.

People living from savings or investments

Self-sufficient applicants may need resources evidence and suitable healthcare cover.

Short stays

Short stays under three months usually follow different rules. This guide focuses on residence registration for longer stays.

NIE vs EU Registration vs TIE

NIEIdentification number used for tax and administrative purposes. It is not automatically proof of residence by itself.
EU Registration CertificateResidence registration for EU citizens. It is usually a green certificate and includes a NIE.
TIEPhysical residence card generally used by non-EU citizens. It is not normally the document EU citizens apply for.
Important

Do not describe EU registration simply as getting a NIE. The NIE is an identification number; EU registration is the residence registration process for EU citizens staying longer than three months.

Main routes to qualify

Working in Spain

You may be asked for an employment contract, employer evidence, work registration or related documents.

Self-employed in Spain

You may be asked for self-employment, tax, activity or Social Security evidence.

Studying

You may be asked for enrolment evidence, healthcare proof and resources evidence.

Retired

You may be asked for pension evidence, healthcare proof such as S1 or suitable insurance, and resources evidence.

Sufficient resources

You may be asked for proof of funds or income and suitable healthcare cover.

Documents you may need

Documents to prepare

  • Passport or national ID
  • Completed EX-18 form
  • Proof of appointment
  • Paid Modelo 790-012 fee
  • Proof of work, self-employment, studies, pension or sufficient resources
  • Healthcare proof, if required for your route
  • Padrón certificate, if requested locally
  • Copies of documents
Requirements vary

Exact requirements can vary by office and personal situation. Check the requirements for your appointment before attending.

Healthcare before EU registration

Why it matters

Some EU citizens may need to show healthcare cover as part of the EU registration file.

Students

Students may need healthcare proof depending on their route and appointment requirements.

Retirees

Retirees may need S1 evidence, private insurance or another accepted healthcare route.

Sufficient resources

People applying with sufficient resources may need suitable healthcare cover before the appointment.

Use the Healthcare in Spain Guide to compare healthcare routes before attending the EU registration appointment.

Important

Healthcare planning may need to happen before your EU registration appointment if your route is not based on employment in Spain.

Appointment and fee

Appointment availability

Appointment availability varies by province and can change over time.

Correct appointment type

Make sure you book the EU citizen registration appointment, not a non-EU TIE appointment.

Fee payment

The official fee is usually paid before the appointment using the relevant fee form.

Fee amount

The current fee is 12.00 EUR, paid via Modelo 790-012. The fee amount can change, so check the current form and instructions before paying.

Important

Do not rely on an old screenshot or forum post for the fee or appointment label. Check the current official instructions before paying or booking.

Real Questions People Ask

Is EU Registration the same as NIE?

No. NIE is an identification number. EU Registration is the residence registration process for EU citizens staying longer than three months, and the certificate usually includes a NIE.

Do EU citizens get a TIE?

Normally no. EU citizens usually receive a green EU Registration Certificate. TIE cards are generally used by non-EU citizens.

Do I need padrón first?

It may be requested locally, depending on the office and appointment requirements. Check before attending.

Do I need private health insurance?

It depends on your route. Workers may use work-related evidence, while students, retirees or self-sufficient applicants may need healthcare proof such as S1 or suitable insurance.

Can I work before registering?

EU citizens can generally enter Spain without a visa, but work, Social Security and registration steps should be handled correctly. Check your employment and registration obligations.

What if there are no appointments?

Appointment availability can vary by province. Keep checking official channels and avoid booking the wrong process out of frustration.

What happens after I receive the certificate?

Keep it safe. You may need it for banking, healthcare, employment, tax, digital access and other administration.

Is the green certificate a card?

It is commonly a green certificate rather than a photo ID card. It is not the same as a TIE.
