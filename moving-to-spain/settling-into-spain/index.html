Settling Into Spain

A calm, practical guide to the first steps after you arrive in Spain. Follow the steps in order, but remember that appointment availability can vary by province and municipality.

Sequence, not deadlines

Quick Answer

After arriving in Spain, the practical sequence usually starts with accommodation and local address evidence, then padrón, healthcare, residence registration if applicable, banking, digital access, Social Security if working, tax review and driving checks. The exact order can shift depending on your situation and local appointment availability.

Timelines vary

The order of these steps matters more than the speed. In some areas, appointments may be available quickly. In others, including high-demand municipalities, waiting times can be much longer. This guide explains what to do next without promising a fixed timeline.

Secure accommodation

Arrival

Your address is often the foundation for later steps, even if your first accommodation is temporary.

Official requirements

Requirements depend on the process using the address evidence. Town halls and other offices may ask for different proof.

Practical advice

Before signing or paying, ask whether the address can support the paperwork you expect to need, especially padrón.

Tip

Keep rental contracts, authorization letters, receipts and host details together so you can compare them with local instructions.

Register on the padrón

Registration

The padrón is municipal address registration at the town hall for the place where you live.

Official requirements

Documents vary by municipality and by housing situation. The town hall decides what evidence it accepts.

Practical advice

Check your town hall process early because appointment availability and accepted documents can vary by municipality.

Use the Padrón Guide when you are ready to prepare documents for this step.

Arrange healthcare

Registration

Healthcare should be understood before any process that asks you to prove cover or entitlement.

Official requirements

Your route may depend on work, self-employment, an S1, student status, private insurance or another entitlement.

Practical advice

Do not assume the same route applies to every newcomer. Healthcare evidence depends on your circumstances.

The Healthcare Guide explains the main routes and common evidence questions.

Register as an EU resident, if applicable

Registration

EU, EEA and Swiss citizens living in Spain longer term usually use the EU Registration Certificate route.

Official requirements

Evidence depends on whether you work, are self-employed, study, retire or live from savings.

Practical advice

Prepare healthcare, address and route evidence before the appointment, and check the exact appointment instructions for your province.

Use the EU Registration Guide if this route applies to you.

Important

EU citizens normally prepare for the EU Registration Certificate, not a TIE. Non-EU family members or other routes may follow different processes.

Open a bank account

Everyday Setup

A Spanish bank account can help with rent, utilities, salary, taxes and local payments.

Official requirements

Banks must identify customers, but accepted documents and account types can vary by bank and by your situation.

Practical advice

Compare fees, document requests and whether the account fits a resident or non-resident situation.

The Bank Account Guide explains what to compare before choosing an account.

Set up Digital Certificate or Cl@ve

Everyday Setup

Digital access helps you use tax, Social Security, municipal and other public-service portals online.

Official requirements

The route you can use may depend on your identity documents, NIE and verification options.

Practical advice

Set this up when your identity details are ready enough for the verification route you choose.

Use the Digital Certificate Guide to compare FNMT digital certificate and Cl@ve options.

Review Social Security, if working

Everyday Setup

If you work or become self-employed in Spain, Social Security registration may affect work, contributions and healthcare entitlement.

Official requirements

Requirements depend on whether you are employed, self-employed or in another work-related situation.

Practical advice

Separate getting a Social Security number from being correctly registered for work or contributions.

If work applies to you, review the Social Security Guide before assuming your employer, client or gestor has completed every step.

Understand tax obligations

Everyday Setup

Tax questions can arise from residence, income, assets, work, self-employment or property.

Official requirements

Tax obligations depend on your circumstances and can involve Spanish and non-Spanish income or assets.

Practical advice

Review your tax position before deadlines or notices become urgent, especially if your situation crosses countries.

The Taxes Guide gives a plain-English starting point for tax residence, tax address and first checks.

Check driving licence rules

Everyday Setup

Driving rules depend on where your licence was issued and whether you are visiting or living in Spain.

Official requirements

Licence validity, exchange or renewal questions can vary depending on your country of issue and residence position.

Practical advice

Check the rules before relying on old assumptions from tourist visits or from another country.

Use the Driving Guide to understand which licence questions to check next.

Common Mistakes

1

Treating the arrival steps as fixed deadlines instead of a sequence that depends on appointments and local rules.

2

Signing accommodation without checking whether it can support padrón or later address evidence.

3

Assuming healthcare evidence is the same for workers, retirees, students and self-funded residents.

4

Booking the wrong residence appointment because the terminology is confusing.

5

Leaving banking, digital access, tax and driving questions until another process is blocked.

Real Questions People Ask

Should I do everything in this exact order?

Use this as the normal sequence, but adapt it to your situation and local appointment availability. Some preparation can happen in parallel.

Can I register on the padrón with temporary accommodation?

It depends on the municipality and the evidence you have. Check the town hall instructions before assuming the address will be accepted.

Do I need healthcare before EU registration?

It depends on your EU registration route. If you are not working, healthcare evidence may be an important part of the registration file.

Is this only for EU citizens?

No. Many arrival steps apply broadly, but EU registration is only relevant if that route applies to you.
