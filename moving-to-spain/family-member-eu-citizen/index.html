Quick Answer

Some non-EU family members of EU citizens may have a specific residence route in Spain, different from ordinary non-EU visa routes. The relationship to the EU citizen and supporting documents are central to the process. Many successful applicants eventually receive a residence card as a family member of an EU citizen. This roadmap helps you understand the broad journey — it does not replace legal advice.

Residence card basics

Family-member residence card

Many non-EU family members receive a residence card connected to their status as family member of an EU citizen.

Different from the EU citizen's certificate

This is different from the EU citizen's own green EU Registration Certificate.

Different from a NIE

It is also different from simply having a NIE, which is an identification number, not a residence card.

Appointments and fingerprints

Card procedures may involve appointments, fingerprints and a separate collection step.

Depends on approval and procedure

The exact process depends on approval and the local procedure used by the office.

Important

Do not confuse the family-member residence card, the EU citizen's registration certificate, and a NIE. Confirm which document applies to your case before an appointment.

Real Questions People Ask

Can my non-EU spouse live with me in Spain?

Often yes, through the family-member route, but it depends on your situation, documents and the relationship recognised by the office handling your case.

Is this the same as a normal non-EU visa?

No. The family-member route is generally distinct from ordinary non-EU visa routes, though details still depend on your case.

Does marriage automatically give residence?

No. Marriage may support eligibility, but residence still depends on the application, evidence and approval.

Is NIE the same as residence?

No. NIE is an identification number. Residence as a family member of an EU citizen is a separate status, usually evidenced by a residence card.

Does the EU citizen need to register first?

It can matter. The EU citizen's own situation in Spain may be part of the family member's case — check the requirements that apply to your situation.

Do we need padrón?

It may be requested as part of the process, depending on the office. Check the requirements for your specific application.

What documents prove the relationship?

Typically a marriage certificate, partnership certificate or birth certificate, depending on the relationship, sometimes with legalisation or translation. Exact needs vary by case.
