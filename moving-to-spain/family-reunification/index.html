Quick Answer

Some people may be able to join family in Spain through a family reunification or family-based residence route. The correct route depends on the family relationship and the residence status of the person already in Spain. This is not the same as the Family Member of an EU Citizen route. Relationship documents, legalisation, translation and proof of circumstances can be central. This roadmap helps you understand the general journey — it does not replace official requirements.

Family reunification vs EU family member route

RouteWhat to know
Family reunificationUsually connected to joining a resident sponsor in Spain. May involve sponsor requirements, and income, housing or residence-status checks. The procedure depends on the family relationship and the sponsor's status.
Family Member of an EU CitizenConnected to an EU citizen's free-movement/family-member route. Different rules and documents may apply. Use the separate Family Member of an EU Citizen Roadmap for this route.
Important

Do not assume these two routes are interchangeable. Confirm which one applies to your situation before preparing documents.

Real Questions People Ask

Can I bring my spouse to Spain?

Often yes, through a family-based route, but it depends on your relationship, the sponsor's residence status, and the documents available. Check the official requirements for your case.

Is family reunification the same as the EU family member route?

No. Family reunification is generally connected to joining a non-EU resident sponsor. The Family Member of an EU Citizen route is a separate, different process — check which one applies to your situation.

Does marriage automatically give residence?

No. Marriage may support eligibility, but residence still depends on the application, evidence and approval.

Can children join family in Spain?

Often yes, depending on the relationship and route, but requirements vary by case. Check the official requirements that apply.

Do family members need a TIE?

Many non-EU family members may need a TIE after approval or arrival. Requirements depend on your route.

Is NIE the same as residence?

No. NIE is an identification number. Residence is a separate status, usually evidenced by a residence card or document.

Do documents need translation or apostille?

Some foreign documents may need official translation, legalisation or apostille, depending on the document and country of issue. Check before applying.

What if the family member is already in Spain?
