Registering on the Padrón in Spain

A practical guide to registering your address with the local town hall, understanding why the padrón matters, and preparing for municipality-specific requirements.

Local rules matter

The padrón is managed by each municipality. Documents, appointments and accepted address evidence may vary by town hall.

Quick Answer

The padrón is the municipal register showing where you live in Spain. It is handled by the local town hall, known as the ayuntamiento. It may be needed for healthcare, residency procedures, schools, local services and other administration. Requirements vary by municipality, so check your local town hall before relying on another person's document list.

At a Glance

What it isMunicipal address registration showing where you live.
Who handles itThe local ayuntamiento for your municipality.
Why it mattersIt may support healthcare, residence procedures, schools, local services and address evidence.
Local variationDocuments and appointment systems may vary by town hall.
Not residencyIt records your local address; it does not grant immigration status.

Who needs it?

EU citizens

EU citizens often need padrón evidence for later local or residence-related steps, depending on the procedure.

Non-EU residents

Non-EU residents may need it for TIE, residence or local administrative procedures, depending on the route.

Families

Families may need address evidence for school, healthcare or local services.

Workers

Workers may need local address evidence for healthcare, employer paperwork or other administration.

Retirees

Retirees may need it for healthcare registration or local services.

Students

Students may need it for local registration, residence-related steps or services.

Anyone living in Spain

Anyone living in Spain and needing local administrative access may be asked for padrón evidence.

Not a permission to live in Spain

The padrón records where you live locally. It is separate from immigration residence permission or EU residence registration.

Why the padrón matters

Healthcare

Regional health services may ask for padrón evidence when you register locally, depending on your healthcare route.

EU registration

EU Registration Certificate appointments may ask for address evidence or a padrón certificate, depending on the office.

TIE and residence procedures

Some TIE or residence-related procedures may ask for local address evidence.

Schools

School registration or local education processes may depend on your registered address.

Local services

Town halls and local services may use the padrón to confirm where you live.

Official address

The padrón can help show your local address for administrative purposes.

Bank or admin processes

Some banks or administrative processes may ask for address evidence, but padrón does not guarantee approval for every service.

Documents town halls may ask for

Common documents to prepare

  • Passport or national ID
  • NIE, if available
  • Rental contract
  • Property deed, if owner
  • Landlord authorisation, if applicable
  • ID copy from the landlord or person authorising registration, if applicable
  • Utility bill, if requested
  • Appointment confirmation, if required
Important

Exact requirements vary by municipality. Check your local ayuntamiento before the appointment.

Before signing a rental contract

Important

Before signing or paying a deposit, ask whether the address can be used for padrón registration and whether the landlord will provide the documents your town hall may request.

Ask before paying

Do this before you pay a deposit or commit to a long-term rental.

Get it in writing

Ask the landlord or agency to confirm what they can provide for padrón registration.

Check the town hall

If you are unsure, ask your local ayuntamiento what evidence they accept for that housing situation.

Appointment availability

Some town halls are fast

Some municipalities may offer quick appointments or straightforward local filing.

Some have long waits

Other town halls may have long waiting times, especially in high-demand areas.

It is not your fault

Long waits or limited appointment slots do not mean you are doing anything wrong.

Check local channels

Use your town hall's current appointment system or contact channel, because availability can change.

No fixed timeline

Appointment availability can vary by municipality. Do not rely on exact timelines unless they are locally verified.

Real Questions People Ask

Is the padrón the same as residency?

No. The padrón records your local address. Residency or residence registration is a separate process.

Can I register with temporary accommodation?

It depends on your municipality, your actual housing situation and the documents the accommodation can provide. Ask the town hall before relying on it.

Can a landlord refuse to help with padrón?

The practical issue is whether the landlord will provide the documents your town hall may request. Ask before signing and keep written confirmation.

Do I need a NIE first?

Not always. Some town halls may accept a passport or national ID, while others may ask for a NIE if you have one. Check locally.

Can I use padrón for healthcare?

It may be part of local healthcare registration, but it does not by itself guarantee healthcare access. Your healthcare route still matters.

Why does my town hall ask for different documents?

Padrón is handled locally, so document requirements may vary by municipality and housing situation.

What if there are no appointments?

Keep checking your local ayuntamiento's appointment system or contact channel. Appointment availability can vary, especially in high-demand areas.
